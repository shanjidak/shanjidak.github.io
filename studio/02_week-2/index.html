Three Significant Changes to the role of a User Experience Designer


1. Embracing Complexity

  • Embrace complexity by including new ways of thinking about design.
  • The Powerful Now is an example of a project between IDEO and SYPartners that is taking on bigger challenges by trying to redefine the experience of aging.
  • “Group-facilitation techniques are becoming required skills to help teams solve complex problems.” User experience designers need collaborators and must learn how to engage with them effectively.

2. Changing the Role of UX Designers
  • Design is becoming more of a compromise between classical design, design thinking, and computational design.
  • UX designers are still getting hired for experience design, but the definition of experience is gaining a broader definition.
  • AirBNB’s cofounder suggests that they look and try to solve their problems through a design lens. Their goal was to design a system to gain their consumer’s trust.



3. Designing Organizations and Processes

  • New ways of thinking and working demand that UX designers “re-imagine how teams and organizations collaborate in support of new endeavors.”
  • Companies are experimenting with new ways to organize work and have employees think in a more holistic manner.
  • “Increasingly, today’s leaders, coaches, and UX designers are confronting the challenges of helping organizations to adopt new ways of working.”
